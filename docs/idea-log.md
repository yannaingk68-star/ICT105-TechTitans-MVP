# Initial IT Venture Idea Log
## Idea 1: PeerTutor – Student-to-Student Tutoring Marketplace
### Problem Area
Rangsit university students experience difficulties in challenging courses but cannot easily find tutors who match their subject, schedule, and budget. As a result, they rely on friends or social media, which is often inefficient and unreliable.
### Target Users
- Rangsit university students who seek academic help.
- Rangsit university students who want to become tutors and earn extra money.
### Current Alternative
- Asking friends or seniors for help.
- Searching for tutors through Facebook groups, Line groups, or word-of-mouth.
- Using expensive private tutoring services.
### Proposed IT Solution
A peer-to-peer tutoring marketplace where students can:
- Create tutor profiles
- List subjects and hourly rates
- Search for tutors by subject
- Book tutoring sessions
- Leave ratings and reviews
- Communicate through an integrated chat system
### Possible Technology
- HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React.js, Python or Java, Firebase or Supabase, PostgreSQL.
- Authentication: University email verification
- Video tutoring: Zoom or Google Meet integration
### Why This is Suitable
This project solves a common student problem and targets users who are easy to access for interviews and testing. It can be developed as a web prototype within one semester using a manageable set of MVP features, while also offering strong potential for future growth and commercialization.

## Idea 2: 
### Problem Area
Tourists visiting Thailand often have difficulty finding authentic local experiences and community-based activities. At the same time, local guides and small tourism businesses struggle to promote their services and reach potential customers, relying mainly on social media or word-of-mouth.
### Target Users
- Domestic and international tourists looking for authentic local experiences.
- Local guides, artisans, and community tourism businesses that want to promote and sell their activities.
### Current Alternative
- Searching through travel agencies, Facebook pages, travel blogs, or online forums.
- Booking popular tours on large travel websites that may not include local community experiences.
- Relying on recommendations from friends or hotel staff.
### Proposed IT Solution
A marketplace platform where local experience providers can:
- Create business profiles
- List tours, workshops, and cultural activities
- Set prices and available schedules
- Receive bookings from tourists
- Collect ratings and reviews
- Communicate with customers through an integrated chat system
### Possible Technology
- HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React.js, Python or Java, Firebase or Supabase, PostgreSQL.
- Authentication: Email or Google account login
- Location services: Google Maps integration
- Booking and review system
### Why This is Suitable
This project addresses a real tourism problem while supporting local communities and small businesses. The target users are accessible for interviews and validation, and the platform can be developed as a web prototype within one semester using core marketplace features such as user registration, listings, booking, reviews, and search functions.
