# Selected Semester Project Opportunity
## Selected Project Title
PeerTutor – Student-to-Student Tutoring Marketplace
## Target User
The primary target users are university students who need academic assistance and students who want to offer tutoring services. Many students look for affordable tutors before quizzes, midterm exams, and final exams, while others have the knowledge and skills to earn extra income by helping their peers.
## Problem Statement
University students often struggle to find suitable tutors for specific subjects. They usually rely on social media posts, chat groups, or recommendations from friends, which can be time-consuming and unreliable. At the same time, student tutors have limited opportunities to promote their services and connect with potential learners.
## Current Alternative
Students currently use Facebook groups, Line groups, or personal recommendations to search for tutors. These methods provide limited information about tutor experience, availability, and reliability. There is also no centralized platform where students can compare tutors or manage tutoring requests efficiently.

## Proposed IT Solution
PeerTutor is a web-based tutoring marketplace that allows students to search for tutors by subject, view tutor profiles, submit tutoring requests, and provide ratings after each session. The platform is designed to make the tutoring process more accessible and organized.
## Why This Opportunity Was Selected
Our team evaluated six IT opportunities using the NUF (New, Useful, and Feasible) method. PeerTutor received the highest score because it solves a common problem, has clearly defined target users, and can be developed as a simple web application within one semester. 
## Why This Is Feasible Without IoT or Advanced Cybersecurity
PeerTutor is a software-based project that does not require hardware devices, sensors, or advanced cybersecurity implementation.
The prototype can be developed using:
- HTML/CSS/JavaScript for the user interface.
- Google Forms for tutor registration and request submission.
- Google Sheets for simple backend data simulation.
#### These technologies are easy to learn, suitable for beginners, and support rapid prototype development within the semester.

## Possible MVP Type
Simple Web App Prototype
The MVP will include:
- Home page
- Tutor listing page
- Tutor registration form
- Tutoring request form
- Basic tutor profile display
#### The MVP focuses on validating the core idea rather than implementing a complete production system.

## Initial Validation Plan for Lab 03
### Target Respondents
- University students who have previously searched for tutors.
- Students who are interested in becoming peer tutors.
### Evidence to Collect
- Current methods used to find tutors.
- Common difficulties when searching for tutoring services.
- Interest in using a university-specific tutoring platform.
- Features students consider most important.
### First Question to Answer
#### "How do university students currently find academic tutors, and what challenges do they experience during that process?"
The collected responses will help determine whether the problem is significant and whether PeerTutor provides a valuable solution for the target users.
